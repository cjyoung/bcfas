UPCOMING PRODUCTIONS

November 9 & 10 @ 8 p.m. and 11 @ 2:30 p.m. • It’s a Wonderful Life: A Live Radio Play – adapted by Joe Landry from the screenplay by Frances Goodrich, Albert Hackett, Frank Capra, and Jo Swerling. This beloved American holiday classic comes to captivating life as a live 1940’s radio broadcast. With the help of an ensemble that brings a few dozen characters to the stage, the story of idealistic George Bailey unfolds as he considers ending his life one fateful Christmas Eve. Director: Cindy Gernand. Tickets are $13/adults; $10/children. Center box office: 979–265–7731, 2–5 p.m. On–line tickets: brazosportcenterstages.org Visa & MasterCard accepted.

AUDITION INFORMATION

December 17 & 18 • 7 p.m. • Auditions for The Diary of Anne Frank – adapted by Wendy Kesselman, Performances: February 15, 16, 22 & 23, 17 & 24 in the Arena theater. In this gripping new adaptation from the original stage play by Goodrich and Hackett, newly discovered writings from the diary of Anne Frank, as well as survivor accounts, are interwoven to create a contemporary, impassioned story of the lives of people persecuted under Nazi rule. Cast needs: Sides available Director: Craig Fritz.

 In the Planetarium Lobby: Space exploration wall with NASA TV, interactive computer stations and displays • ViewSpace: Images from Hubble, Spitzer & Chandra telescopes with the latest news about our solar system, updated daily by astronomers at the Space Telescope Science Institute in Maryland. • Gift shop items include the latest in astronomy books, supplies & gifts for all ages. Lobby is open to the public at no charge while school is in session, Tuesday – Friday, 10 a.m. – 4:30 p.m. and before Planetarium Shows.

PLANETARIUM SHOWS

November 6, 13, 20 & 27 @ 7 p.m. • The Wildest Weather in the Solar System • New Full Dome show for the entire family by National Geographic. Come on a spectacular journey Packed with eye-popping visuals, high-end CGI, and cutting-edge science, to witness the most beautiful, powerful, and mysterious weather phenomena in the solar system. From a storm the size of a 100-megaton hydrogen bomb, to a 400-year-old hurricane, to a dust tempest that could engulf entire planets, you'll be glad you live on Earth! Audiences will fly through the thick atmosphere of Venus, magnetic storms on the sun, liquid methane showers on Titan, and anticyclones whirling at hundreds of miles per hour on Jupiter. Co-sponsored by Brazosport Rotary. Also, Tonight’s Sky • What’s over your house tonight? Tickets available 20 minutes before the show. $5 Adults, $3 Students. Call 979–265–3376 for information.

December 4, 11 & 18 (No show December 25) • 7 p.m. • Season of Light • Herald the holiday season with this well–loved program that has been reformatted for the Full Dome. Share some of the warmest and brightest celebrations of the year and look into some theories about the Christmas star. Also, Tonight’s Sky • What’s over your house tonight? Tickets available 20 minutes before the show. $5 Adults, $3 Students. Call 979–265–3376 for information.

BRAZOSPORT MUSEUM OF NATURAL SCIENCE

 Rated one of the ten best museums in Texas by Frommer’s Travel Guide. Largest collection of shells on display in the South with shells from around the world. Life–size dinosaur casts; amazing wildlife; exciting fossils; glamorous gems & minerals; fascinating archeology; hands–on children's area piques children’s interest in the natural world. Displays change frequently. Visit www.bmns.org or call 979–265–7831 for more information. Open Tuesday–Saturday • 10 a.m.–4 p.m. and Sunday • 2–5 p.m. and during Center Stages productions. Free admission.
