PLANETARIUM SHOWS

March 5, 12, 19 & 26 and Friday, March 15 • 7 p.m. • The Wildest Weather in the Solar System • New Full Dome show by National Geographic. Come on a spectacular journey Packed with eye–popping visuals, high-end CGI, and cutting-edge science, to witness the most beautiful, powerful, and mysterious weather phenomena in the solar system. From a storm the size of a 100–megaton hydrogen bomb, to a 400–year-old hurricane, to a dust tempest that could engulf entire planets, you'll be glad you live on Earth! Audiences will fly through the thick atmosphere of Venus, magnetic storms on the sun, liquid methane showers on Titan, and anticyclones whirling at hundreds of miles per hour on Jupiter. Co-sponsored by Brazosport Rotary. Also, Tonight’s Sky • What’s over your house tonight? Tickets available 20 minutes before the show. $5 Adults, $3 Students. Call 979–265–3376 for information.

BRAZOSPORT MUSEUM OF NATURAL SCIENCE

 Rated one of the ten best museums in Texas by Frommer’s Travel Guide. Largest collection of shells on display in the South with shells from around the world. Life–size dinosaur casts; amazing wildlife; exciting fossils; glamorous gems & minerals; fascinating archeology; hands–on children's area piques children’s interest in the natural world. Displays change frequently. Visit www.bmns.org or call 979–265–7831 for more information. Open Tuesday–Saturday • 10 a.m.–4 p.m. and Sunday • 2–5 p.m. and during Center Stages productions. Free admission.

MUSEUM AFFILIATES

March 5, April 2, & May 7 • 7 p.m. • Sea Shell Searchers of Brazoria County • Meetings, the 1st Tuesday of every month in the Museum, are open to the public. Bring a covered dish to share. For more information, see www.seashellsearchers.org

March 12, April 9, & May 14 • 7:30 p.m. • Brazosport Archaeological Society • Meetings, in the Museum, are open to the public. Join them in their study of the prehistory and history of the lower Brazos River region. The Society is a non-profit organization created to stimulate an active interest in the discovery, conservation, and salvage of archaeological sites, and the recording and preservation of archaeological remains using scientific procedures. For more information, visit www.bmns.org.

March 25, April 22, & May 27 • 7 p.m. • Brazosport Birders and Naturalists • Meetings, the 4th Monday of every month in the Museum, are open to the public. All those interested in birding and conservation of our natural resources are welcome. For more information, visit their page on Facebook.
