BRAZOSPORT PLANETARIUM

 In the Planetarium Lobby: Space exploration wall with NASA TV, interactive computer stations and displays • ViewSpace: Images from Hubble, Spitzer & Chandra telescopes with the latest news about our solar system, updated daily by astronomers at the Space Telescope Science Institute in Maryland. • Gift shop items include the latest in astronomy books, supplies & gifts for all ages. Lobby is open to the public at no charge while school is in session, Tuesday – Friday, 10 a.m. – 4:30 p.m. and before Planetarium Shows.

PLANETARIUM SHOWS

June, Tuesdays and Fridays • 7 p.m. • Overview • The transforming view of Earth from Space. Travel out from the earth and look back at our stunning, fragile planet. Narrated by NASA Astronauts. Also, Tonight’s Sky • What’s over your house tonight? Tickets available 20 minutes before the show. $5 Adults, $3 Students. Call 979–265–3376 for information.

July, Tuesdays and Fridays • 7 p.m. • Wildest Weather • Full Dome show by National Geographic. Come on a spectacular journey Packed with eye-popping visuals, high-end CGI, and cutting-edge science, to witness the most beautiful, powerful, and mysterious weather phenomena in the solar system. From a storm the size of a 100-megaton hydrogen bomb, to a 400-year-old hurricane, to a dust tempest that could engulf entire planets, you’ll be glad you live on Earth! Audiences will fly through the thick atmosphere of Venus, magnetic storms on the sun, liquid methane showers on Titan, and anticyclones whirling at hundreds of miles per hour on Jupiter. Co-sponsored by Brazosport Rotary. • Also, Tonight’s Sky • What’s over your house tonight? Tickets available 20 minutes before the show. $5 Adults, $3 Students. Call 979–265–3376 for information.

August 6, 9, 13 & 16 • 7 p.m. • Astronaut • Narrated by Ewan McGregor and produced by the National Space Centre in Leicester, England. An adventure unlike anything you’ve ever encountered. Meet the animated test astronaut Chad and discover the perils that lurk in space from his viewpoint! Explore the amazing worlds of both inner and outer space, from floating around the International Space Station to maneuvering through microscopic regions of the human body as you experience the chain of events inside the body of an astronaut during a rocket launch. • Also, Tonight’s Sky • What’s over your house tonight? Tickets available 20 minutes before the show. $5 Adults, $3 Students. Call 979–265–3376 for information.

Shows take place every Tuesday evening each month • Future shows are to be announced, all shows inclued Tonight’s Sky • What’s over your house tonight? Tickets available 20 minutes before the show. $5 Adults, $3 Students. Call 979–265–3376 for information.

BRAZOSPORT MUSEUM OF NATURAL SCIENCE

 Rated one of the ten best museums in Texas by Frommer’s Travel Guide. Largest collection of shells on display in the South with shells from around the world. Life–size dinosaur casts; amazing wildlife; exciting fossils; glamorous gems & minerals; fascinating archeology; hands–on children's area piques children’s interest in the natural world. Displays change frequently. Visit www.bmns.org or call 979–265–7831 for more information. Open Tuesday–Saturday • 10 a.m.– 4 p.m. and Sunday • 2–5 p.m. and during Center Stages productions. Free admission.

July 3 • 4:30 p.m. • Volunteer Ice Cream Social • A cool way for Museum volunteers to relax and enjoy each other’s company! Spouses and prospective volunteers are welcome.

MUSEUM AFFILIATES

May 7 • 7 p.m. • Sea Shell Searchers of Brazoria County • Meetings, the 1st Tuesday of every month in the Museum, are open to the public. Bring a covered dish to share. For more information, see www.seashellsearchers.org

July 9 & August 13 • 7:30 p.m. • Brazosport Archaeological Society • Meetings, in the Museum, are open to the public. Join them in their study of the prehistory and history of the lower Brazos River region. The Society is a non-profit organization created to stimulate an active interest in the discovery, conservation, and salvage of archaeological sites, and the recording and preservation of archaeological remains using scientific procedures. For more information, visit www.bmns.org.
